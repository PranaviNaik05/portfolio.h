Urban Planning and Development

Urban planning involves designing and managing city infrastructure, housing, transportation, and public spaces to ensure sustainable development. Our project aims to resolve critical urban challenges like overcrowding, traffic congestion, and environmental degradation by implementing innovative solutions. We focus on creating efficient, inclusive, and eco-friendly spaces that enhance the quality of urban living. Tools like 2D arrays, DFS, and Dijkstra’s algorithm can be utilized.

Why use these tools?

  • DFS: Used for exploring and analyzing connected components in city layouts, such as identifying regions, ensuring accessibility, and determining feasible development zones.
  • Dijkstra's Algorithm: Ideal for finding the shortest paths in transportation or utility networks, optimizing routes for emergency services, traffic management, and resource distribution.
Economic Disruption Management

Economic distribution and management near Indraprastha city, located by the Kosi River, face challenges like uneven resource allocation, inefficient logistics, and underdeveloped infrastructure. These issues hinder equitable growth, disrupt trade, and affect livelihoods. Addressing these requires innovative strategies for sustainable development, efficient resource utilization, and improved socio-economic systems.

Probable tools: Kruskal’s algorithm, queues, and arrays.

Why use these tools?

  • Kruskal's Algorithm: Used for optimizing resource distribution networks, such as minimizing transportation costs or connecting multiple warehouses with the least expense (Minimum Spanning Tree).
  • Queues: Efficient for managing task scheduling, like prioritizing shipments or customer service requests.
  • Arrays: Help store and manipulate large datasets, such as inventory levels, transportation costs, or demand forecasts.
